Web Evolution


A brief history

Assessment 4

In the beginning - Welcome HTML

In the 1990s, we welcomed the first publishing language of the Web: Hypertext Markup Language, or HTML. But the language – used to share text-only pages via a simple browser – was limiting. Many early web sites were basic, using vertically structured, text-heavy pages with few graphics.

1990

The rise of Internet Explorer, Amazon and Ebay

What we know today as eCommerce had a historic milestone that year, the emergence of the giant Amazon and Ebay, companies that since then are dominant in the eCommerce market operating in several countries around the world. With the advances of the internet, microsoft also launches the internet explorer, a world-famous browser. Many brave users were still breaking through all the territories of this technology called internet. That's exactly where the name “Explorer” comes from, as it refers to “exploring”.

1995

Javascript is created!

Year that marked the creation of Javascript, a programming language used in more than 95% of existing websites on the Web. It was initially developed in a simpler way as it is used today, being accepted by browsers such as Netscape and Internet Explorer.

1996

The emergence of social networking

Around the 2000s, the internet had a significant increase in presence at work and at people's homes. With this, social networks leveraged an immense mass of users and from that period onwards, a multitude of services emerged. In 2002, Fotolog and Friendster were born, bringing photo-sharing options and becoming very popular especially among young people at the time. Then, throughout 2003, LinkedIn (focused on professional contacts) and MySpace were launched. Here we have arrived at the time when social networks fell in favor of internet users and turned into money machines, 2004 can be considered the year of social networks, because during this period Flickr, Orkut and Facebook were created.

2000 - 2004

The giant arises - Google

The company's stated mission from the beginning was "to organize the world's information and make it universally accessible and useful". Google began as an online search firm, but it now offers more than 50 Internet services and products, from e-mail and online document creation to software for mobile phones and tablet computers. Google actually operates in 219 countries!

2004

Everything is in the cloud

Between this period several cloud service providers were created, among them the market dominant Microsoft azure and AWS (Amazon Web Services). This process revolutionized what we know today related to technology, enabling people and companies to save data and use services remotely, securely and at a reduced cost. Cloud services have grown 27% in 2021 comparing to the previous year.

2006 - 2008

Mobile Web Age

Mobile web takes over, more websites now develop mobile versions of sites and Apps. Certainly many large companies with large budgets were able to create and program their own mobile websites and even smartphone apps.

2008

Internet 5G - Welcome to the future

The use of 5G promises to be a definite leap for the future. The speed of 5G is up to 100 times faster than the wireless systems used today. 5G wireless technology is meant to deliver higher multi-Gbps peak data speeds, ultra low latency, more reliability, massive network capacity, increased availability, and a more uniform user experience to more users.
